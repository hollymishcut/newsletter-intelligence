a16z explicitly named this as an unsolved problem. Protege is launching a DataLab, but no company has built a standardized, trusted framework for measuring and certifying training data quality. As data becomes the bottleneck (not compute), whoever sets this standard wins an infrastructure-level position — the way credit scores own financial risk assessment.
What the ideal company looks like:
API-first data audit tool → certification badge for datasets → marketplace of certified training data. Moat is trust + network effects from enterprise AI buyers requiring certified data.

CGMs, Apple Watch, AR glasses are everywhere. Engineers from Nvidia, Apple, and Google are showing up to wearables hackathons in droves. But there is no trusted, regulated AI layer that turns consumer wearable data into clinical-grade insight. The hardware exists. The demand exists. The analysis layer doesn't — and it's the highest-margin part of the stack.
What the ideal company looks like:
B2B2C: sell to health systems and insurers who embed into patient-facing apps. Revenue model: per-patient per-month. Moat is FDA clearance + proprietary longitudinal data.

B2B (employer-pays) reskilling platform with AI career path mapping. Moat is employer partnerships + placement rate data. Comparable to Lambda School model but for mid-career transitions into AI ops roles.
